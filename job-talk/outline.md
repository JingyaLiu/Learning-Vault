# Job Talk Outline

Industry job talks ≠ academic faculty talks. Shorter, one thread, less "survey of my PhD."

**Target length:** 25–35 min + Q&A  
**Core rule:** One flagship direction, not every project you ever touched.

---

## Talk Title


**One-sentence thesis:** What should the audience remember?

---

## Slide Outline

| # | Slide | Time | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Title + who you are (10 sec) | 0:30 | No long bio |
| 2 | Problem — why anyone should care | 2:00 | Hook with failure of status quo |
| 3 | Key insight / hypothesis | 2:00 | Your unique angle |
| 4–6 | Method (flagship work) | 10:00 | One main figure per slide; build incrementally |
| 7 | Results — numbers + visual | 4:00 | Compare to strong baselines |
| 8 | Analysis / ablation — what we learned | 3:00 | Shows depth |
| 9 | Brief tie-in: 1–2 related projects | 3:00 | Only if same thread |
| 10 | Open problems + what I'd do at [company] | 3:00 | Forward-looking |
| 11 | Thank you / backup slide pointer | 0:30 | |

## Rehearsal Checklist

- [ ] Timed run ≤ 30 min
- [ ] Can explain main figure without reading slide
- [ ] Backup slides for: extra ablation, architecture detail, related work
- [ ] Practice interruption: stop at slide 4, explain method cold
- [ ] Record video; watch at 1.25× — cut filler words

## vs Academic Job Talk

| Academic | Industry RS/MTS |
|---|---|
| Broad CV coverage | One coherent thread |
| 45–50 min | 25–35 min |
| Future faculty plan | What you'd build here |
| Teaching slide | Usually omit |
