# Negotiation Playbook

Read this **once** before your first recruiter call. Execute when offers arrive.

Initial offers leave room by design. Recruiters often say: *"I don't expect you to take our first offer."*

---

## Before Any Recruiter Call

Write down (do not improvise live):

| Topic | My policy |
|---|---|
| Share other processes? | Yes, vague timeline / no company names |
| Share competing offers? | Only after written offer; range not exact |
| Share current comp? | Usually deflect; "looking for market rate for role" |
| Earliest start date | |
| Must-haves (level, team, remote) | |
| Walk-away number | (private — do not share) |

---

## Scripts

### Buying time
> "Thank you — I'm excited about this. I'm finishing a few other processes and want to make a thoughtful decision. Can we target [date + 1–2 weeks] for a decision?"

### They ask for competing offer details
> "I have another offer in a similar band and timeline. I'd like to understand what flexibility you have on [base / level / sign-on / equity]."

### They push for immediate answer
> "I take this decision seriously. I won't be able to commit before [date]. If that's a hard constraint, please let me know now so we can both plan."

### After verbal yes on numbers
> "Great — I'll look for the written offer with the full breakdown. I'll review and get back to you by [date]."

---

## What to Negotiate (typical ML RS package)

- [ ] Level / title (often biggest lever)
- [ ] Base salary
- [ ] Sign-on bonus
- [ ] Equity refresh / grant size
- [ ] Start date / vacation before start
- [ ] Team placement (sometimes pre-commit in writing)

Ask peers + levels.fyi + blind (salt heavily) for bands.

---

## Post-Offer Phase

Expect: teammate chats, manager 1:1s, office visits, many emails.

- Block calendar time for "offer management" like a part-time job
- Respond within 24–48h even if just "thanks, reviewing"
- Teammate calls are ** mutual eval** — ask about on-call, promotion, project kill rate

---

## Emotional Notes

- Outmatched on market knowledge is normal — borrow friends' scripts
- "I'd be happy at the initial number" ≠ skip negotiation
- Guilt about asking is not a reason to leave money on the table
- Ghosting happens; track and move on

---

## Checklist When Offer Arrives

- [ ] Written offer in hand (not verbal only)
- [ ] Compare: base, bonus, equity, vest schedule, cliff
- [ ] 2+ data points on level for that company
- [ ] Script for recruiter call written
- [ ] Decision deadline negotiated
- [ ] Talked to 1 trusted friend who negotiated recently
